about the rally


Monte Carlo Rally


Location:Monte Carlo
Date:17 - 20 January
Terrain: Asphalt Rally
Weather: Changing conditions
Overview:



The most famous rally of them all, this is the event everyone wants to win. Based among the chic shops and expensive restaurants of Monte Carlo itself, the action takes place on asphalt roads in the French Alps. The rally is more compact than ever with most of the competition in the mountains directly above the Principality - the famous ascent to the Col de Turini will be tackled four times. Drivers must expect snow, ice and dry asphalt - often encountering all three on the same special stage. Shrewd tyre selection providing the best compromise for frequently changing conditions is the key to success.



Ford's Track record:

In the overall 2001 season, Colin McRae set the most fastest times winning 35 stages; the Ford Focus RS WRC set the most fastest times, being quickest on 80 special stages, and Ford spent a total of 61 stages leading rallies.Colin McRae spent six stages leading the Monte Carlo Rally in 2001. The Monte Carlo Rally 2002 ran three stages in darkness and there were also five new special stages (plus 4 repeats).The predicted start order for Ford Rallye Sport drivers for the Monte Carlo Rally was: McRae second, Sainz fifth and Martin fifteenth.


What happened last year

Carlos Sainz / Luis Moya - 3rd Colin McRae / Nicky Grist - 4th Markko Martin / Michael Park - 12th We made a strong start to the 2002 FIA World Rally Championship by finishing third and fourth on the Monte Carlo Rally. Carlos Sainz and Luis Moya took the final podium place in their Ford Focus RS World Rally Car while Colin McRae and Nicky Grist were just one place behind. The three-day rally, fought out on twisty asphalt roads high in the French Alps, was one of the mildest for many years. Snow was virtually non-existent and the roads alternated between wet and dry with only a few ice patches to trap the unwary. The classic roads acted as a magnet to fans and huge crowds lined the speed tests to see the worlds best drivers in action.
